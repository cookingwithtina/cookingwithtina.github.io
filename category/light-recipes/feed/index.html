]]> Black Rice with veggies, shrimp and zucchini is a light, colorful and flavorful first course that is simple to make and perfect for a summer lunch or buffet, an outing, in short, for all occasions! This tasty and delicate dish, rich in nutritional properties thanks to the Venus rice, is made with shrimp, peas, cashews and your favorite vegetables, everyone really likes it, and it is also very beautiful to look at!

Venus rice contains anthocyanins, flavonols and phenolic acids, phenolic compounds known to have antioxidant properties; contains insoluble fiber, vitamins and minerals such as selenium, iron, calcium, zinc and manganese, it also has low sugar

Ingredients for 3/4 persons

  • 300 gr Black Venus Rice
  • 100 gr Frozen Shrimps
  • one zucchini
  • half a leek
  • 200 gr frozen peas
  • two tomatoes
  • 100 gr roasted cashews
  • to taste Extra-virgin olive oil
  • to taste Salt, pepper
  • a tablespoon of soy sauce
  • fresh basil

First begin by cooking the Venus Rice. Bring plenty of water to a boil, salt it and pour in the black rice. Follow the cooking time indicated on your Venus Rice package, about 40 to 45 minutes, then drain the rice and set aside.

Boil the frozen shrimp for 5/6 minutes, then set aside; do the same with the peas, which you will boil 10 minutes.

Brown the chopped leek in a wok-type pan with two tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil.

Add the chopped zucchini and cook, over medium heat, for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and pepper.

Also add the chopped tomatoes, shrimp, peas and finally cashews; add the soy sauce and let it evaporate.

Finally add the rice, stir and serve the venus rice directly in the pot with a few leaves of fresh basil, you will smell it!

]]> Vegan Carrot Doughnut Cake is simple, fragrant and delicious, very quick to prepare, light and fluffy! This cake with the scent of oranges, maple syrup and almonds is made without any ingredients of animal origin, so without eggs, milk and butter, but it is nothing like my Camilla Cake! It is really wholesome and perfect for the whole family, for breakfast or snack; ideal for people who are lactose intolerant, those who follow a vegan diet, those who are on a diet or simply those who love all the goodness of a healthy carrot cake!

  • 250 g raw carrots
  • 150 gr organic whole wheat mixed cake flour (or mixed brown and rice flour)
  • 50 gr of pearl and chopped almonds
  • 50 gr potato starch
  • 100 gr of brown sugar
  • 150 ml lukewarm water (or soy or rice or oat milk)
  • 150 ml orange juice
  • orange zest
  • two tablespoons of maple syrup (or agave syrup, or date syrup, which you can substitute with a teaspoon of vanilla essence)
  • 150 ml of seed oil
  • grated peel of 1 orange
  • 1 sachet of baking powder
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • powdered sugar to complete

First clean and peel the carrots, place them in a blender along with the orange peel and ‘seed oil and blend 5 – 6 minutes until you get a smooth cream (this is the secret to a super soft carrot cake!) Mix the warm water, sugar, maple syrup and orange juice in a bowl; in a separate bowl mix the flour, starch, ground almonds, sugar, pinch of salt and baking powder

Slowly add the flours with baking powder to the liquids, stirring with a spatula; then add the cream of carrots to the mixture, mixing the mixture well. Pour the mixture into an oiled and floured 18- to 20-cm baking dish or lined with baking paper and bake in a preheated static oven at 180째 for 40 minutes (it is always worth doing a toothpick test).

Remove from the oven and let it cool at least 20 minutes before serving; you can present the Vegan Carrot Cake with a little maple syrup on top-a real treat!

]]> My vegan avocado, banana and chocolate brownies are truly amazing, mouthwatering treats that are no match for classic brownies! They are made with simple ingredients such as banana, peanut butter, avocado, dates and maple syrup; all mixed with bitter cocoa and chocolate chips! They are sweet, yet full of wholesome, healthy nutrients, egg-free, dairy-free, and butter-free! The texture is amazing: every bite melts in your mouth. If you want to make a gluten-free version, instead of 00 flour you can use half almond flour and half rice or corn flour!

  • 100 g dark chocolate chips
  • 1 large, ripe avocado
  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 3 pitted dates
  • 60 gr 00 flour (alternatively 30 gr whole wheat flour and 30 gr almond flour)
  • 20 g cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder for cakes
  • 30 gr peanut butter (alternatively, chop roasted peanuts with a tablespoon of seed oil)
  • 30 gr of brown sugar
  • half a teaspoon of salt
  • 5/6 walnuts, or two tablespoons of hazelnuts of your choice

Line a square or rectangular baking pan with baking paper. Blend together the avocado, bananas and dates in a blender and whisk until it forms a smooth mass. Finally add the chocolate chips and nuts (your choice) and mix with a spatula. Pour the mixture into the mold and bake for 45 minutes in a preheated oven at 180째, until the top starts to brown and the inside is cooked (you can pierce it with a toothpick, it should come out wet, but without dripping batter).

Let it cool completely before removing it from the mold and cutting. You can serve the vegan Brownies with maple or date syrup!

]]> Pineapple, yogurt and ginger smoothie is a thirst-quenching, refreshing, digestive and nutritious drink made with pineapple, white yogurt, fresh ginger and turmeric. This diet shake, rich in vitamins and minerals, is perfect any time of day, for breakfast, as a snack or to end a meal.

 

Pineapple is an exotic draining and digestive fruit, a source of organic acids (malic acid, citric acid) and trace elements (calcium, phosphorus, magnesium, potassium; rich in vitamin C and beta-carotene; contains bromelain, an enzyme found in the stem that has an anti-inflammatory action. Ginger is a spice that has an important antioxidant action and protects the skin from free radicals. Turmeric is a spice known for its antioxidant, immune-stimulating, anti-arthritic, digestive and choleretic properties.

 

Serves 2 people

 

  • 380 g fresh pineapple pieces
  • 1 piece of fresh ginger root
  • 125 g white yogurt
  • a pinch of turmeric powder
  • juice of 1/2 lemon

 

Clean the pineapple by removing the rind and the stringy center, then cut it into pieces and collect them in the glass of a blender.

 

 

Peel the ginger root, cut it into pieces and place it inside the blender. Add the yogurt, turmeric and lemon juice; blend at high speed until you get a creamy mixture; pour into two glasses and serve immediately.

]]> Coffee Frosted Coconut Cake is a soft and luscious cake, fragrant and inviting, without gluten, butter or oil, perfect for those who feel like indulging in a treat without too many calories! This soft, fresh and tasty cake. is perfect at all times of the day, for breakfast, snack, or as an original end of the meal.

 

  • 200 g coconut milk (150 g for the cake and 50 g for the coffee icing)
  • 1 banana
  • 150 g egg white (from about 4 eggs)
  • 50 gr brown sugar
  • 120 g buckwheat flour (which you can change to 60 g buckwheat flour and 60 g rice flour)
  • 40 gr of gluten-free oatmeal, which you can replace with coconut flour
  • 20 gr of cornstarch
  • one sachet of vanillin
  • zest of half a lemon
  • 8 gr baking powder for cakes
  • 20 gr of bitter cocoa powder
  • 15 gr liquid bitter mocha coffee
  • 20 gr date syrup or maple syrup

 

Start the preparation of the coconut cake by mashing the banana well with a fork, then add 150 g of coconut milk and egg white and mix well with an electric whisk.

 

 

Then add the dry ingredients, then the sugar, buckwheat flour, oats or coconut flour, vanillin, lemon zest and baking powder; mix well until smooth, pour onto a buttered and floured cake pan 18 cm in diameter and bake in a preheated static oven at 180째 for 25/30 min.

 

 

Meanwhile prepare the coffee glaze: combine the cocoa, coffee and syrup inside a bowl and mix until a thick glaze forms; store in the refrigerator.

 

 

When the cake is ready take it out of the oven, let it cool completely and decorate it with the coffee frosting, store in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

 

 

For a gluten version you can substitute coconut and buckwheat flours with 00 flour; you can substitute coconut milk with Greek yogurt.

For a vegan version try substituting egg whites for another mashed banana, and let me know how the cake turns out!

]]> Linguine with swordfish and cherry tomatoes is a light, tasty and impressive fish first course, worthy of great occasions, despite the fact that it is really quick and easy to prepare. This colorful and richly flavored and fragrant first course is suitable for all seasons, also perfect for a large dinner party when you have little time in the kitchen but still want to bring an appetizing dish to the table.

 

Ingredients for 4 people

 

  • 360 gr of linguine (or spaghetti)
  • 300 gr of swordfish
  • 200 gr of cherry tomatoes
  • 40 ml dry white wine
  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 tuft of parsley
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

 

Start the preparation by cleaning the swordfish slices from the skin and cut them into cubes.

 

 

Pour and heat the oil in a large wok-type pan, add the chopped onion and let it cook 3 minutes; then add the cherry tomatoes cut in half and let it cook two minutes.

 

 

Add swordfish cubes, stirring for one minute, deglaze with wine and let evaporate. Add salt and pepper and parsley and finish cooking another 2 minutes.

 

 

Meanwhile, cook the pasta in plenty of boiling, salted water and, once the cooking time indicated on the package has elapsed, drain and toss with the swordfish sauce.

 

 

Serve immediately.

]]> Icelandic Cheesecake is a cold, no-bake, creamy and delicate dessert made quickly and easily. This delicious and light cake is made using Skyr, a soft cheese typical of Iceland; it is perfect for a fresh and original end to a meal, incorporated into a travel-themed dinner! If you love, as I do, traveling with food, also try my recipe for Icelandic Fish Stew, or look for the dish you like best in the “Ethnic and International Recipes” section!

 

Skyr is a delicious dairy product typical of Icelandic cuisine, but can also be found in Italy in the best supermarkets. Its appearance and texture are similar to Greek yogurt, but it is actually richer in protein, and contains less fat and sugar.

 

Doses for an 18/20 cm diameter mold:

 

  • 150 g Digestive cookies
  • 60 gr of melted butter
  • 400 gr of blueberry- or raspberry-flavored Skyr (or if you prefer, plain)
  • 250 gr of fresh liquid cream
  • 30 gr of powdered sugar
  • 10 gr gelatin sheets or isinglass
  • blueberry jam

 

Line the base of the mold with baking paper. Soak the gelatin sheets in cold water.

 

Blend the digestive cookies in a mixer, add them to the melted butter and mix to make a sandy mixture.

 

Pour into the mold and compact it well with your hands, then place the mold in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

 

 

Whip 200 ml of the cold cream with the powdered sugar and set aside; put the remaining 50 g of cream in a small saucepan and heat it, then turn off the heat, add the well-wrung gelatin sheets, stir and let cool.

 

 

In a large bowl put the skyr and stir, add the gelatin dissolved in the cream and mix well. Now add the whipped cream and mix, stirring from top to bottom.

 

 

Pour the mixture over the chilled base and let it firm up in the refrigerator for at least 5 hours (preferably overnight).

 

Before serving, decorate with blueberry jam, or fresh blueberries and serve.

]]> Almond and Cinnamon Cake is a very quick, tasty dessert that is perfect for breakfast or as a dessert at the end of a meal. This soft and delicious cake is made with almond flour, eggs, sugar, lemon and Amaretto di Saronno, which gives it a very soft and moist texture in the bite, to melt in your mouth! Ideal to serve cold, when all the ingredients have settled, with a dusting of powdered sugar, perhaps accompanied by a scoop of ice cream! The cake contains no gluten, so it is also suitable for people with celiac disease!

 

  • 250 g finely chopped peeled almonds (or almond flour)
  • 4 eggs
  • 150 g sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch or potato starch
  • grated peel and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon powder
  • 30 ml amaretto di saronno (or limoncello, or orange juice)
  • 1 sachet of baking powder

 

Start the preparation by separating the yolks from the egg whites, whip the latter until stiff with an electric beater and set aside.

 

 

Then whip yolks with sugar, lemon zest and juice, and cinnamon, for 5 minutes on high speed until frothy and light. Pour in the ‘amaretto (or limoncello or orange juice), continue whipping. Add the almond flour, cornstarch and baking powder and mix the mixture with a spatula from the bottom up. Then add the egg whites until stiff, gradually adding them with a spatula so as not to disassemble the mixture.

 

 

Finally pour into a buttered and floured mold, or a cake pan 20 cm in diameter, then bake in a preheated static oven at 180째 for 35 minutes

 

 

Remove from the oven and let cool before serving, with a dusting of powdered sugar.

]]> Grilled Eggplant is an easy, light, quick and tasty vegetable side dish made with slices of eggplant grilled and then seasoned with a fragrant marinade of oil, garlic, mint and embellished with olives. This flavorful dish is perfect to pair with any dish, such as grilled meat or fish, with cheeses and cured meats or; you can serve it as an appetizer, to stuff sandwiches or crostini. Also, once consumed, you can use the remaining oil to dress pasta!

 

  • 3 eggplants
  • extra virgin olive oil to taste. (about two cups)
  • 100 g green or black olives, pitted
  • 3/4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 hot peppers
  • coarse salt
  • pepper to taste
  • mint leaves

 

Begin preparation by slicing the eggplant with a long, sharp knife or a mandoline to a thickness of about 4 to 5 mm.

 

 

Heat a grill well, add a little coarse salt and begin grilling the eggplant slices, for about 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until the black stripes typical of well-grilled vegetables appear. As you grill, add a little more coarse salt to the grill.

 

 

Meanwhile, prepare the high-sided baking dish where you will put the eggplant: on the bottom put a little olive oil, a couple of mint leaves, a couple of pieces of fresh garlic, pepper.

 

 

Lay the grilled eggplant slices in the marinade, so that they fill the bottom. Add on top of the eggplant layer some more oil, garlic, olives, pepper, and if you like a chili pepper too!

 

 

Intersperse with more eggplant, and proceed this way, in layers, until you run out of ingredients. Go ahead and abound with the ‘olive oil, then you will reuse it to season the pasta, nothing goes to waste in the kitchen! In the ‘last layer add olives, garlic and mint.

 

Finally let them marinate for at least 30 minutes before serving, even better if you let them sit in the fridge overnight! You can serve your mouthwatering eggplant cold as well!

 

You can alternatively use a glass jar, always proceeding in layers, perfect for gifting eggplant to friends and family!

]]> Savory tuna is a simple, quick and tasty recipe; a soft fish main course that is easy to prepare, so it is great for when you do not have much time to devote to cooking. This dish made with slices of fresh tuna, flavored with oregano and garlic and cooked in a pan, quickly and easily, is also light, perfect even for those who follow a low-calorie diet, but do not want to give up taste. Follow my recipe and prepare Tuna savory, and accompany it with a mixed salad, or seasonal vegetables. If you love this fish also try my Tuna with Marsala, with sesame, or the one with peas!

 

Ingredients for two people:

 

  • 2 x 150 g slices of fresh tuna
  • 2 tablespoons chopped oregano
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a tablespoon of flour
  • a small glass of white wine

 

Clean the tuna slices, discard the skin and bones, season them with a tablespoon of oregano, a teaspoon of garlic, a pinch of salt and a tablespoon of oil; cover and let them season for at least an hour in the refrigerator.

 

 

Drain the tuna slices and flour them lightly; wilt the remaining oregano and garlic with a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet, add the tuna and let it brown a few minutes on both sides.

 

 

 

Then deglaze with the wine and finish cooking for another 4 minutes, then season with salt and pepper and serve at the table.
